Turbo 80

Turbo 80 is a third-person fast-paced team-based true-3D vehicular combat game set in a retro-futuristic world which perfectly captures the vibe of the 80s action and sci-fi movies.

The game puts you in control of a specially designed flying vehicle which has different types of weapons mounted on it. There are four general classes of vehicles in the game, each encouraging a different play style:

The combat system features two teams consisting of six players each with various vehicle types fighting for multiple points of interest on the map.

The levels in Turbo 80 can be generated completely procedurally. I have written an algorithm for generating cities that have various building types which can adapt to a procedurally generated terrain. Within the city I have devised production of flying vehicle traffic that is programmed to follow special rules of the so-called “true-3D space”.

The prototype of Turbo 80 has an AI system written from scratch, and is able to navigate in non-planar 3D space (unrestricted up-down forward-backward left-right movement) which makes it distinctive from similar games in the genre. It has 100 levels of difficulty that you can choose from – the lowest being almost passive and the hardest being impossible to beat.

Codename: CueBeat

Codename: CueBeat is a story-driven first-person puzzler set in communist Yugoslavia. You are an electromagnetism expert lead by a barely detectable radio transmission to a secret military magnet testing facility that looks like someone's home. There you have to solve a set of puzzles involving special kinds of magnets in order to be accepted into the experimentation team.
